SABERTOOTH

A lightweight, powerful Inspection Maintenance & Repair (IMR) autonomous vehicle made by SAAB SeaEye, a part of SAAB group The Sabertooth is ideal for a variety of missions: inspection, repair and maintenance, research tasks and environmental monitoring.

Operated remotely or autonomously in deep, cold waters, maneuvering with or without a tether.

Configured in double or single hull allowing it to operate up to 3000m below the surface, the Sabertooth docks at a base station maintenance-free for up to 6 months, where it launches on pre-programmed or person-controlled missions.

SEARCH FOR ENDURANCE

A pair of Sabertooths was instrumental in the search for Sir Henry Shackleton's ship Endurance, sunk in 1915 to a depth of 3008m.

Researchers succeeded in locating, examining, and filming the wreck thanks to the vehicles' endurance, robustness, advanced control system and high-tech equipment, including an array of cameras, sonars, and communication.

The Saberooth's autonomous functionality, 6D movement, coverage, stability and speed allows the Sabertooth to deliver better data, quicker, and with fewer operators.
